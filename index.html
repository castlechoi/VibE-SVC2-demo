VibE-SVC II: Vibrato Expression Control for Singing Voice Conversion with Improving Inference Time Control

 

Joon-Seung Choi, Dong-Min Byun, and Seong-Whan Lee

Abstract

Singing technique is a crucial aspect of expressive singing voice. In this paper, we introduce VibE-SVC II, a singing voice conversion framework that offers expanded and disentangled control over complex singing techniques. For pitch techniques, we introduce an energy style converter that models the volume modulations associated with vibrato, significantly improving conversion performance. Our framework also enables vibrato rate control at inference time and supports a novel method for MIDI-based pitch conditioning without using MIDI during training. For timbre techniques, we extend the model to handle a variety of timbre techniques. To address the unique challenges of vocal fry which is characterized by subharmonics, we propose a novel Subharmonic Correction (SHC) algorithm that refines the F0 contour for more natural timbre conversion. Through comprehensive objective and subjective evaluations, we demonstrate that VibE-SVC II provides fine-grained, independent control over a wide range of singing techniques, outperforming existing methods.
